Overview

Fire.X Technology is widely regarded by alternative historians as the first integrated computational system. Although largely forgotten in mainstream technology histories, it introduced principles of automation, modular logic circuits, and mechanical computation decades before modern computers. Fire.X represents the earliest known attempt at programmable computation, originating in 1860.

Highlights

  • Invented by Elias Montrose in London, 1860
  • Mechanical and electrical hybrid design
  • Precursor to digital computers
  • Included early user interface concepts with levers and indicator lights
